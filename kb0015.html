Purchase Request

To create a purchase request, follow these general steps:

  1. Identify the Need: Determine what item or service you need to purchase and why. Consider factors such as quantity, quality, specifications, and budget constraints.

  2. Obtain Approval: If required by your organization's policies, obtain approval for the purchase request from the appropriate stakeholders, such as a manager or department head.

  3. Gather Information: Collect all relevant information about the purchase, including product details, pricing, vendor information, and any special requirements or considerations.

  4. Fill Out Purchase Request Form: Most organizations have a standardized purchase request form or template that you'll need to fill out. Provide all necessary information on the form, including:
    • Description of the item or service to be purchased
    • Quantity needed
    • Estimated cost or budget allocation
    • Vendor information (if known)
    • Justification for the purchase
    • Any special instructions or requirements

  5. Submit Purchase Request: Once the purchase request form is complete, submit it to the appropriate department or individual responsible for procurement, such as the purchasing department or procurement manager. This may involve submitting the form electronically through an online system or physically delivering it to the relevant department.

  6. Follow-Up: After submitting the purchase request, follow up with the procurement department or relevant stakeholders to ensure that the request is being processed promptly. Provide any additional information or clarification as needed.

  7. Review and Approval: The procurement department will review the purchase request to ensure compliance with organizational policies and procedures. Depending on the organization's approval process, the request may need to be approved by multiple parties before a purchase order is issued.

  8. Receive Purchase Order: Once the purchase request is approved, a purchase order (PO) will be issued to the selected vendor authorizing the purchase. Keep track of the PO number and any other relevant information for future reference.

  9. Receive Goods or Services: Once the vendor receives the purchase order, they will fulfill the order by delivering the goods or providing the requested services.

  10. Review and Confirm Receipt: Upon receiving the goods or services, review them to ensure they meet your expectations and specifications. Confirm receipt of the items by signing any delivery receipts or acknowledgment forms, if required.

By following these steps, you can effectively create and submit a purchase request within your organization's procurement process. Be sure to adhere to any specific procedures or guidelines established by your organization for purchasing and procurement.
