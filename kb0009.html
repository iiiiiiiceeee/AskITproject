Reset WiFi Network

To reset your Wi-Fi network settings in Windows, you can follow these steps:

  1. Open Network Settings: Right-click on the network icon in the system tray (it looks like a series of bars or a globe) located in the bottom right corner of the taskbar. Then, select "Open Network & Internet settings" from the menu.

  2. Access Wi-Fi Settings: In the Network & Internet settings window, select the "Wi-Fi" tab from the left-hand sidebar.

  3. Manage Known Networks: Scroll down and find the "Manage known networks" section. This is where you can view and manage the Wi-Fi networks your computer has connected to in the past.

  4. Forget Wi-Fi Network: Find the Wi-Fi network you want to reset and click on it to expand the options. Then, select "Forget" to remove the network from your list of known networks. This will delete the network's saved settings, including the password.

  5. Reconnect to Wi-Fi Network: After forgetting the Wi-Fi network, you'll need to reconnect to it as if it were a new network. Click on the network icon in the system tray, select the Wi-Fi network you want to connect to and enter the password if prompted.

  6. Optional: Reset Network Settings: If you're experiencing broader network connectivity issues and want to reset all network settings in Windows, you can do so through the Settings app:

    1. Open the Settings app by pressing the Windows key + I.

    2. Go to Network & Internet > Status.

    3. Scroll down and click on "Network reset".

    4. Click on "Reset now" to confirm. This will reset all network adapters on your computer, including Wi-Fi, Ethernet, and Bluetooth. Your computer will restart after the reset is complete.

By following these steps, you can reset your Wi-Fi network settings in Windows to resolve connectivity issues or start fresh with a clean network configuration.
