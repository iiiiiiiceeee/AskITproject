How to enable Two-Factor Authentication

To enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) in Gmail you can follow these steps:

Sign in to your Gmail account: Go to Gmail and sign in with your username and password.

Access your Google Account settings:

Click on your profile picture located at the top right corner. Then click on "Google Account".

Navigate to Security:

In the left sidebar select "Security".

Find Two-Step Verification:

Scroll down until you find the "Signing in to Google" section. Look for the option called "2-Step Verification" or "Two-Step Verification".

Start the setup process:

Click on "2-Step Verification". You might be prompted to enter your password again to confirm your identity.

Verify your phone number:

Google will ask you to verify your phone number. This is the number where you will receive codes to access your account. Select your country code and enter your phone number. Choose how you want to receive verification codes: via text message or phone call.

Enter the verification code:

Once you receive the code on your phone enter it into the box provided on the screen and click "Next".

Confirm the setup:

After entering the code Google will confirm that 2-Step Verification is turned on for your account.

Alternative methods for 2FA:

Google also provides backup codes that you can download or print which can be used if you don't have access to your phone. You can also set up authenticator apps like Google Authenticator or Authy to generate verification codes.

Done:

Once the setup is complete you'll be asked to re-enter your password and then you'll see a confirmation that 2FA is enabled. After setting up 2FA whenever you or someone else tries to sign in to your Gmail account from an unrecognized device or location you'll be asked to enter a verification code sent to your phone providing an extra layer of security.
